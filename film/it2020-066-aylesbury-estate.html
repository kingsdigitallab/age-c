Synopsis

Aylesbury Estate, the largest public housing complex in London, built in the 1960s on the rubble of old public housing in the South East, is now undergoing a new epochal change. Carlotta Berti offers an intimate and profound portrait of a resilient community, showing from within the struggle of the residents who defend their homes from attempts at speculation.
