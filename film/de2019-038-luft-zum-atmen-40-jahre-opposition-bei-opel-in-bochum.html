Luft Zum Atmen - 40 Jahre Opposition Bei Opel In Bochum

2019

In 1972, a few workers at Opel in Bochum founded the ‚ÄúGroup of Oppositional Trade Unionists‚Äù. The GoG existed for over 40 years and fueled the spirit of resistance among the Bochum workforce with its radical factory work. As works councillors, they passed on secret information to the workforce, organized eight-hour works meetings, fought against sick leave, organized their own educational leave and attempted to establish international solidarity between the GM workforces in Europe on their own initiative in order to defend themselves against the site blackmail in the 1990s. Their activities culminated in the most important wildcat strike in German post-war history, when the workforce occupied the plant for six days in October 2004 and paralyzed production throughout Europe. - Discussion with the filmmaker and a former Opel worker afterwards!
