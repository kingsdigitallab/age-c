Synopsis

"Will the Holocaust soon be as far away for young people as the Thirty Years' War or the Battle of the Teutoburg Forest?" This question does not arise for the seven children and grandchildren of high-ranking Nazi perpetrators and Holocaust survivors who, in "Bequest", talk about their repressed feelings of guilt and trauma as well as the liberating confrontation with this legacy.
